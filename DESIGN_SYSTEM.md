We are building a nonprofit web application called "Bonfire" that serves as a 
case management and donor engagement platform for an organization that protects 
and rehabilitates trafficking survivors. The name "Bonfire" evokes warmth, 
safety, community, and light in the darkness.

Apply a consistent design system across the entire application with the 
following specifications:

BRAND IDENTITY
- Name: Bonfire
- Tagline concept: "Light in the darkness" / "Where healing begins"
- Tone: Warm, safe, trustworthy, hopeful — never clinical or cold
- Avoid: harsh colors, sharp geometric aesthetics, corporate/sterile feel

COLOR PALETTE
- Primary: A deep warm amber/orange (#C2621A or similar) — used for buttons, 
  active states, and key CTAs
- Secondary: A rich terracotta or burnt sienna (#A0522D or similar) — used for 
  accents and hover states
- Background: A warm off-white or cream (#FDF6EE or similar) — never pure white
- Surface/Card: A soft warm white (#FFF8F2 or similar) with subtle warm shadow
- Dark text: Deep warm charcoal (#2C1A0E or similar) — never pure black
- Muted text: Warm medium brown (#7A5C44 or similar)
- Success: Soft sage green (#6B8F6B or similar)
- Warning: Warm golden yellow (#D4A017 or similar)
- Danger/Alert: Muted brick red (#B94040 or similar)
- All colors should feel like they belong around a campfire — warm, earthy, 
  grounded

TYPOGRAPHY
- Heading font: A friendly serif or semi-rounded sans-serif (e.g., Lora, 
  Playfair Display, or DM Serif Display) — warm and approachable, not 
  corporate
- Body font: A clean, highly readable sans-serif (e.g., Inter, DM Sans, 
  or Nunito) — comfortable for long-form reading
- Use generous line height (1.6–1.8) for body text
- Headings should feel warm and human, not institutional

COMPONENT STYLING
- Border radius: Generously rounded (12–16px for cards, 8px for inputs, 
  full pill for badges)
- Shadows: Soft, warm-tinted box shadows (not cold gray shadows) — 
  e.g., rgba(180, 80, 20, 0.08)
- Cards: Warm white background, subtle warm border 
  (1px solid rgba(180,100,40,0.12)), rounded corners, soft shadow
- Buttons (primary): Amber/orange fill, white text, rounded, warm hover 
  darkening — no harsh transitions
- Buttons (secondary): Transparent with warm border, amber text
- Inputs: Warm off-white background, warm border, rounded, focus ring 
  in primary amber
- Nav/Sidebar: Deep warm brown or charcoal background with amber accents 
  for active items — feels like a cozy interior, not a cold admin panel
- Badges/status pills: Soft, muted warm tones — avoid neon or harsh colors
- Tables: Alternating warm cream rows, no harsh grid lines
- Icons: Use Lucide React icons throughout for consistency

IMAGERY & ILLUSTRATION DIRECTION
- If placeholder images are needed, use warm-toned gradients 
  (amber → terracotta)
- Avoid stock photo aesthetics — prefer illustrated or icon-based UI elements
- Any data visualizations (charts, graphs) should use the warm color palette

LOGO PLACEHOLDER
- Use a simple flame or bonfire SVG icon in amber/orange next to the 
  wordmark "Bonfire" in the heading font
- Keep it minimal and clean

LAYOUT & SPACING
- Use generous whitespace — the site should feel open and breathable, 
  not cluttered
- Max content width: 1200px centered
- Consistent padding: 24px on mobile, 40px on desktop
- Sidebar navigation for authenticated admin/staff views
- Clean top navigation bar for public-facing pages

ACCESSIBILITY
- Maintain WCAG AA contrast ratios on all text/background combinations
- Ensure the warm color palette does not sacrifice readability
- All interactive elements must have visible focus states
- at least 90% lighthouse accessibility score on each page.

Apply this design system consistently across all pages including the public 
landing page, admin dashboard, case management pages, donor management pages, 
and all forms. The overall feeling should be: a user opens the app and 
immediately feels like they are in a safe, warm, trustworthy space.
